About

After several years in pursuit of a doctorate in Philosophy I became intrigued by the conceptual overlap between my field of graduate study and software design. I had never been "tech-y", but after teaching myself core Java and becoming completely enthralled with the satisfaction of watching something I had built go to work, I concluded my studies and began taking "soft skill" IT roles while continuing to self-teach, as work and family life allowed.

I continue to do so, and I believe I'll never stop. I love the thrill of developing... the design, the head-scratching, the epiphanies, the quandaries, the hunt for the solution, and of course the completed, working code!

There is nothing like it!

As I continue to build my development portfolio (see below), I continue to be interested in a wide range of IT roles, from technical support to business analysis.
